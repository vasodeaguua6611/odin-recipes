Aji de Gallina

Ready for some creamy, spicy dish? El Aji de Gallina is a weird Peruvian chicken stew that's like a warm hug for your taste buds! This dish combines shredded chicken in a rich, creamy sauce made with aji amarillo peppers, bread, milk, and walnuts. It's comfort food with a Peruvian twist!

Ingredients

  • 1 whole chicken breast, poached and shredded
  • 4 slices white bread, crusts removed
  • 1 cup milk (for soaking the bread)
  • 1/2 cup walnuts, ground
  • 3 tablespoons aji amarillo paste (our beloved yellow pepper!)
  • 1 onion, finely chopped
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1/2 cup grated Parmesan cheese
  • 2 cups chicken broth (from poaching the chicken)
  • 4 yellow potatoes, boiled and sliced
  • 3 hard-boiled eggs (because eggs make everything better!)
  • Black olives for garnish

Instructions

  1. Soak the bread in milk until soft and mushy (about 10 minutes). Then blend until smooth.
  2. In a large pot, saut√© onion and garlic until the onion is translucent and soft.
  3. Add the aji amarillo paste and cook for another 3 minutes, stirring constantly (don't let it burn!).
  4. Add the bread mixture, ground walnuts, and chicken broth. Stir well and cook for about 10 minutes.
  5. Add the shredded chicken and Parmesan cheese. Cook until the sauce thickens to your desired consistency.
  6. Serve hot over rice and sliced yellow potatoes.
  7. Garnish with hard-boiled egg slices and black olives (this is not optional - it's tradition!).

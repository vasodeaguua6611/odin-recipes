Ingredients

  • 1 pound beef tenderloin, cut into strips
  • 2 red onions, sliced into thick wedges
  • 3 tomatoes, cut into thick wedges
  • 3 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 2 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon red wine vinegar
  • 2 cups french fries (fresh or frozen)
  • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil
  • 1 aji amarillo (yellow chili pepper), sliced
  • Fresh cilantro for garnish
  • White rice for serving (because double carbs = double happiness!)

Instructions

  1. Prepare your french fries first - either fry them yourself or bake frozen ones until crispy. Set aside but keep warm.
  2. Heat oil in a large wok or skillet over high heat until smoking. Add the beef strips in small batches and sear until browned (about 2-3 minutes per batch). Remove and set aside.
  3. In the same wok, stir-fry the onions and aji amarillo until the onions start to soften. Add garlic and cook for another minute.
  4. Add tomatoes and cook just until they start to release their juices but are still firm.
  5. Return the beef to the wok, add soy sauce and vinegar. Toss everything together quickly.
  6. Add the french fries and cilantro, give it one final toss (careful not to break the fries!)
  7. Serve immediately over rice while everything is hot and sizzling!
